## Context

This blog post is a ChatGPT response to an analysis I was carrying out.

I used ChatGPT to analyse a series of images I created, examples of other people's artwork, and a series of outputs generated by analysing several hundred Instagram images alongside scraped SEO data. The goal was to identify and establish a style guide — something that would first identify the artistic direction I wanted to take, and then build it in a way most likely to resonate with the audience most receptive to it.

The response below is reproduced exactly as it was generated by ChatGPT during that analysis.

---

> ## Custom Video Production Approach: Maximising Your Artistic Identity
>
> Your artistic identity—rooted in structured chaos, horror surrealism, decay, neon aesthetics, and unsettling contrast—calls for a bespoke video production approach. Given your interest in airbrushing, stop-motion, mixed media, After Effects, and Houdini, the strongest approach is a hybrid workflow that balances tactile and digital elements.
>
> ---
>
> ### 1. Core Video Style: “Digital Decay & The Occult Glitch”
>
> Your work already plays with distortion, texture, and unsettling contrast. Your video approach should amplify these themes through:
>
> 🔹 Textural erosion & decay (both practical & digital effects)  
> 🔹 Glitch horror & corrupted movement  
> 🔹 Uncanny human-like figures & unnatural animation  
> 🔹 Neon-washed grime—aesthetic tension between beauty & rot  
> 🔹 Surreal, physics-defying compositions  
>
> To achieve this, your workflow should incorporate procedural digital effects, airbrushed practical elements, and carefully structured motion design.
>
> ---
>
> ### 2. Primary Workflow: Hybrid Practical-Digital
>
> #### Step 1: Tactile Elements & Raw Materials
>
> Since your airbrushing and physical texture work is already a strength, integrate this into video through:
>
> ✔ Stop-motion elements: Mask designs, textured paintings, or models animated frame-by-frame.  
> ✔ Airbrushed or hand-painted distortions: Create ghostly, organic distortions over footage.  
> ✔ Scanned decay: Scan cracked, splattered, or distorted paint to use as texture overlays.  
> ✔ Physical glitching: Corrupt printouts of your work, then scan them for analog digital degradation.  
>
> 🔹 Why? This grounds your work in something tangible, enhancing its psychological impact.
>
> ---
>
> #### Step 2: Procedural Digital Enhancements
>
> Once physical elements are captured, bring them into After Effects & Houdini for surreal animation.
>
> **🟠 After Effects (Motion Control, Post-Processing, Compositing)**
>
> ✅ Controlled Glitch & VHS Decay: Use displacement maps & databending to create flickering, warped visuals.  
> ✅ Typographic Horror: Animating text in a distorted, layered, or corrupted manner.  
> ✅ Time Distortion: Use frame blending & interpolation artifacts to create eerie, half-formed motion.  
> ✅ Neon-Lit Darkness: Selective colorization (bright, toxic neon tones layered over deep shadows).  
> ✅ Layered Texture Mapping: Overlay paint textures, brush strokes, and decay effects onto moving subjects.
>
> 🔹 Why? This adds thematic depth while keeping the tactile work intact.
>
> ---
>
> **🟣 Houdini (Procedural Horror, Decay, and Unnatural Movement)**
>
> ✅ Flesh & Decay Simulations: Particle systems for eroding, melting, or corrupting visuals.  
> ✅ Physics-Defying Growth: Create veins, glitching tendrils, or organic structures growing procedurally.  
> ✅ Surreal Weightlessness: Design floating, gravity-defying horror compositions.  
> ✅ Smoke & Distortion Fields: Use volumetrics and procedural fluid effects to create ghostly, inhuman atmospheres.  
> ✅ Digital Body Horror: Apply soft-body physics to warp and distort organic forms.
>
> 🔹 Why? Houdini brings an extra layer of complexity, making movement unnatural, ethereal, and disturbing.
>
> ---
>
> ### 3. Experimental Aesthetic Techniques (Pushing the Boundaries)
>
> To ensure your work stands out, you should experiment with:
>
> **💀 Corrupting Reality**
>
> • Datamoshing: Use compression artifacts to create haunting, broken movement.  
> • AI Interference: Train models on your distorted artwork, then animate these outputs.
>
> **🔥 Hybrid Stop-Motion & Digital Augmentation**
>
> • Digitally animate a static painting, making elements shift subtly.  
> • Use frame-rate blending to mix real and digital motion seamlessly.
>
> **🎭 Uncanny Human Movement**
>
> • Frame interpolation errors to create unsettling motion (e.g., ghostly afterimages).  
> • Warping facial features using displacement mapping.
>

> ✔ Horror-infused music videos or animated sequences for underground artists.  
> ✔ Longer, immersive pieces with heavy atmosphere and evolving decay.

> ### 5. Conclusion
>
> Your best approach combines:
>
> ✅ Tactile, airbrushed, and stop-motion elements → grounding the work in realism.
