This site documents a personal system for applying **machine learning to content strategy and SEO**, tested through a body of grotesque, surreal, and technically experimental (but otherwise shitty) artwork.

It is not a product or service.  
It is a working environment—used to develop, test, and deploy a methodology across real pages.

---

## 🔷 What This Site Is

A **modular, content-driven website** that demonstrates:

- Programmatic SEO deployment  
- Semantic clustering and internal linking  
- Automated metadata generation  
- Iterative content refinement under real constraints  

The artwork is not decoration.  
It functions as **difficult subject matter**, used to test whether the system can extract structure and generate coherence in unstable or unconventional domains.

---

## 🔧 Technical Core

- **Reusable Site Structure**  
  One underlying logic; content and design vary without changing system behaviour  

- **Markdown-Driven Content**  
  Pages are generated from structured files rather than hardcoded layouts  

- **Incremental Static Regeneration (ISR)**  
  Content updates dynamically without requiring full redeployment  

- **Metadata Automation**  
  File watchers (e.g. `chokidar`) generate and update metadata in real time  

- **SEO Implementation**
  - Topic clustering and entity grouping  
  - Programmatic internal linking  
  - Automated metadata derived from LLM summarisation  

---

## 🧠 Methodological Framework

The system is built around a personal methodology referred to as **Emergent Structural Mapping (ESM)**.

Its function is to:

- Extract structure from noisy or unorganised inputs  
- Identify redundancy, suppression, and adjacency  
- Apply pressure through iteration and recompression  
- Retain only what remains coherent under constraint  

Outputs are treated as **artefacts under pressure**, not final answers.

This methodology is applied across all pages on the site.  
Documentation and implementation examples are available throughout the project.
