# Custom Video Production Approach: Maximising Artistic Identity

An artistic identity built on structured chaos, horror surrealism, decay, neon aesthetics, and unsettling contrast requires a bespoke video production approach. A hybrid workflow that merges tactile and digital elements—particularly airbrushing, stop-motion, mixed media, After Effects, and Houdini—offers the most coherent path forward.

---

## 1. Core Video Style: “Digital Decay & The Occult Glitch”

This visual identity thrives on distortion, texture, and contrast. Video execution should amplify these through:

- Textural erosion and decay (practical and digital)  
- Glitch horror and corrupted movement  
- Uncanny humanoid figures and unnatural animation  
- Neon-washed grime—aesthetic tension between vibrancy and rot  
- Surreal, physics-defying compositions  

A structured workflow should incorporate procedural digital effects, airbrushed textures, and precision motion design.

---

## 2. Primary Workflow: Hybrid Practical–Digital

### Step 1: Tactile Elements and Raw Materials

Airbrushed and physical texture work can be directly integrated via:

- Stop-motion elements: Masked figures, painted textures, or physical models  
- Hand-painted distortions: Ghostly overlays created with physical media  
- Scanned decay: Cracked, splattered, or corrupted textures scanned for digital use  
- Physical glitching: Damaged printouts re-scanned to simulate analogue degradation  

**Purpose**: Grounds digital work in tangible matter, enhancing psychological weight.

---

### Step 2: Procedural Digital Enhancements

Captured material can be imported into After Effects and Houdini for surreal extension and refinement.

#### After Effects (Motion Control, Post-Processing, Compositing)

- Controlled glitch effects via displacement and databending  
- Typographic distortion for psychological impact  
- Time warping through frame blending and artefact layering  
- Neon and shadow contrast: high-saturation overlays on deep blacks  
- Texture overlays: Integrating paint and decay into motion

**Purpose**: Maintains tactile presence while deepening thematic structure.

#### Houdini (Procedural Horror, Decay, Unnatural Motion)

- Decay simulations using particles and erosion shaders  
- Procedural growth of veins, tendrils, and glitch-organic structures  
- Gravity-defying motion for dreamlike horror sequences  
- Volumetric fog and distortion for inhuman ambience  
- Soft-body deformation for digital body horror

**Purpose**: Enables unnatural, surreal motion with deep structural control.

---

## 3. Experimental Aesthetic Techniques

### Corrupting Reality

- Datamoshing for broken, haunted movement  
- AI-driven animation using models trained on distorted artwork  

### Hybrid Stop-Motion & Digital Augmentation

- Static paintings animated digitally with shifting elements  
- Frame-rate blending between real and procedural motion  

### Uncanny Human Movement

- Frame interpolation artefacts for ghost-trailing effects  
- Facial feature warping via displacement and mesh distortion  

---

## 4. Resonant Content Types

Formats that maximise impact and audience engagement:

- Short-form experimental videos (10–60 sec) for social media  
- Seamless looping animations for Instagram and TikTok  
- Horror-themed music videos or visuals for underground artists  
- Longer atmospheric sequences with progressive degradation  

---

## 5. Conclusion

Effective execution requires:

- Physical textures and airbrush work for grounding  
- Motion typography and glitch layering via After Effects  
- Procedural decay and transformation via Houdini  
- Experimental workflows blending AI, frame interpolation, and databending  

This approach leverages both analogue and digital media to form a distinct and unsettling visual identity—merging decayed beauty, structured distortion, and surreal horror into a singular form.
